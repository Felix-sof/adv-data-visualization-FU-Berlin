Conclusion
Preference: Color scheme
yelloworangered — with viridis as accessibility fallback

Yelloworangered encodes the data's semantics directly: yellow = diluted / effective, deep red = high dose needed / resistant. This maps onto natural warning conventions (traffic lights, heat alerts), reducing cognitive load. Its main weakness is protanopia — the red endpoint loses saturation. Where accessibility is critical, viridis is the better choice: perceptually uniform by design and robust under all four major color vision deficiencies. Rainbow should be avoided for sequential quantitative data — its non-monotone luminance creates a false mid-range peak and fails badly under color-blindness simulation.
